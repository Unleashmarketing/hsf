What Data We Collect

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on the following legal grounds under GDPR:

Contract Performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR)

Processing necessary for the performance of recruitment services and placement contracts.

Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR)

For business development, quality improvement, and maintaining client relationships.

Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR)

Where you have explicitly consented to specific processing activities, such as marketing communications.

Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR)

To comply with employment law, tax obligations, and regulatory requirements.

Special Category Data: Medical and health information is processed only with your explicit consent under Art. 9(2)(a) GDPR or where necessary for employment purposes under Art. 9(2)(b) GDPR.

Data Retention

Active Candidates

Personal data of active candidates is retained for the duration of our recruitment relationship and up to 2 years after successful placement for support purposes.

Unsuccessful Applications

Data from unsuccessful applications is retained for up to 1 year unless you request earlier deletion or consent to longer retention for future opportunities.

Placed Professionals

For successfully placed professionals, we retain contact and placement information for up to 7 years for ongoing support and compliance purposes.

Legal Requirements

Some data may be retained longer to comply with legal obligations, such as:

  • Employment records (up to 10 years)
  • Tax and financial records (up to 10 years)
  • Immigration documentation (as required by law)

Your Rights Under GDPR

Access (Art. 15 GDPR)

You have the right to obtain confirmation that we process your personal data and access to that data.

Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)

You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)

You may request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.

Restriction (Art. 18 GDPR)

You can request restriction of processing in specific situations.

Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR)

You have the right to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

Objection (Art. 21 GDPR)

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw consent at any time.
